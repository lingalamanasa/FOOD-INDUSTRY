Our Journey of Ag-Defense

Stackly AgSec was established in 2018 with a simple, high-stakes directive: to design biosecurity frameworks that protect crop outputs and safeguard global distribution loops. What began as a local pilot testing project has scaled into an internationally recognized hub of food safety innovation.

Every sensor we deploy and audit we conduct is focused on mitigating risks. We combine solar-powered IoT micro-sensors, predictive regional diagnostics, and temperature tracking systems to ensure food supply stability from field to table.

Our approach integrates cutting-edge genetic crop analysis with traditional agricultural engineering practices to design a defense network that is both highly resilient and cost-effective.

Our Technology Ecosystem

We merge three layers of technical safeguards to build a secure food supply infrastructure.

IoT Crop Telemetry

Solar telemetry arrays placed directly in the soil send moisture, temperature, and electrical conductivity metrics every 15 minutes to our cloud hub.

Regional Risk Prediction

Machine learning algorithms process regional climate changes and micro-weather patterns to forecast pest wave movements and soil drying risks.

Pathogen DNA Profiling

Using rapid PCR diagnostic testing, we identify soil pathogens and mold strains, drafting targeted remediation strategies before crops are affected.
